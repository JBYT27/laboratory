At Stony Brook University, members of the Kim laboratory study complex host-pathogen-vector interactions with a specific focus on tick-borne pathogens, such as Rickettsia and Powassan virus (in collaboration with Dr. Mackow).


Our current research projects are,

1. Identify rickettsial genes important for tick-borne rickettsiosis by characterizing kkaebi transposon variants.

2. Discover diverse R. amblyommatis genetic traits and their associations to unique phenotypes infecting ticks and mammalian hosts.

3. Survey local tick populations and their impacts on the prevalence of tick-borne diseases.

4. Understand molecular mechanisms associated with POWV neurovirulence and host immunity.


Collectively, we aim to characterize the underlying molecular mechanisms enabling pathogenic microorganisms to cause acute and chronic diseases and study host-pathogen-vector interactions using molecular genetic and microscopic tools, tissue culture assays, animal infection models, and clinical and field samples.
